Strawberry picking

Strawberry picking is a quintessential summer activity where people visit local farms,
gather in rows of sun-kissed strawberry plants,
and pluck ripe berries using a gentle twisting motion.
It's a delightful outdoor experience,
allowing families and friends to enjoy the fresh air while collecting strawberries for various culinary delights.
The picked berries often find their way into homemade jams,
desserts, or are savored on their own.
It's a simple yet joyful tradition that encapsulates the essence of summer's bounty.

Medicinal properties & nutritional values

Anti-inflammatory activity

In several studies in which subjects were asked to consume a strawberry drink or a drink containing freeze-dried strawberries after a meal, a decrease in inflammatory markers was found following the consumption of strawberries compared to a placebo (drink or food without strawberries).

Maintaining weight

The strawberry has a low caloric density - that is, a relatively large volume for a low amount of calories, partly due to the high fiber content. The low caloric value (32 calories per 100 grams) along with the health benefits and the indulgent taste of the strawberry make it the perfect snack for those who want to lose weight or maintain it. 250-200 grams of strawberries constitute a portion of fruit - and this is a relatively large amount of fruit.
